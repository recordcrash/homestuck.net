Official Homestuck Works

A list of the official works of the Homestuck franchise, with descriptions and links.

Free Works

Homestuck

A tale about a boy and his friends and the reality-altering game they play together. Now archived in its original version by Bambosh's Unofficial Homestuck Collection, the best way to read it.

Originally, there was a Homestuck Beta purely on flash, abandoned three days later after it became too painful to make.

Read Homestuck

The Homestuck Epilogues

Non-canon, black comedy/meta followups to the original ending of the comic. Made by a couple of Hussie's friends after he gave them a loose outline. Designed to be a potentially alienating "offramp" for fans, in that fans are meant to take them as irrelevant if they wish and just make their own endings instead.

Read Epilogues

Homestuck: Beyond Canon

Homestuck^2, a non-canon official sequel to the Epilogues by a team of bad actors, was cancelled in 2020 due to internal issues and quality concerns.

In late 2023, it relaunched as Homestuck: Beyond Canon, with a new team that aligns closer to the original comic's style and engages more positively with fans. They also made some changes to the old pages.

Read Beyond Canon

MS Paint Adventures

Formerly the main franchise worked on by Andrew Hussie, Homestuck is part of and associated to the works in it:

  • Problem Sleuth: Initially a parody of detective noir stories, turned sci-fi and took the plot in wackier and zanier directions. Considered by many to be Hussie's magnum opus.
  • Blood Spade: Just an easter egg referring to the fact Midnight Crew is an adventure in the Homestuck universe. Did you know there was going to be a Midnight Crew adventure game?
  • Bard Quest: An unfinished 47-page experiment on why branching paths are a bad idea.
  • Jailbreak: The origin of MS Paint Adventures and many running jokes like pumpkins, had a rushed joke ending in 2011 after being abandoned in 2007.

Sweet Bro and Hella Jeff

Originally spawned by Andrew Hussie from an exchange on the Penny Arcade forums, it became a comic drawn by Dave Strider within Homestuck. Highly ironic and dangerous humor is contained within.

Read SBAHJ

Ryanquest

Ryanquest is a completely legitimate and in no way sarcastic or silly adventure starring Ryan North and Andrew Hussie, inspired by a comment North made on Dinosaur Comics.

A mysterious prelude known only as vigilprince also exists.

Read Ryanquest

MS Paint Adventures Bonus Commands

You have unlocked some FAN-REQUESTED GAME COMMANDS. These are drawn and posted on mspaintadventures if you make a DONATION! The origin of the Midnight Crew as a concept, Hussie used to make these, but no longer has time for them.

Read Commands

Character Social Network Accounts

As time went on and Homestuck started referencing more real social networks for some reason, Hussie decided making actual accounts for a couple of characters would be a good idea.

  • Dave Strider's Blogspot: Surprisingly obscure and never linked within the comic itself, this blog contains a few articles written in character about highly ironic subjects like Tony Hawk.
  • I-AM-YOUR-LORD: Caliborn's DeviantArt account, containing all his best man gaka drawings.
  • @thevriskaserket: Vriska's Instagram, containing all the art from her unfortunate return to life.
  • mspaofficial: A Snapchat account for the entire cast, released some teasers for the Homestuck Credits and Hiveswap ACT 1, but also some later-decanonized stories about Earth C. No longer official.

Skaianet Systems

Also known as the Cursed History, this site appears as an in-universe website for Skaianet, the company in Homestuck, with a map of meteor strike locations, a countdown to 4/13/29. By viewing the source code a number of hidden links could be found relating a number of canonical facts about the history of the Condesce and Earth A. Originally archived by Skyplayer in Homestuck.info and re-archived by us.

View Skaianet Systems

Paradox Space

Paradox Space is a cancelled Homestuck spinoff comic series, launched in 2014. It was described by Hussie as containing stories with no overarching narrative, some AUs, some doomed timelines, some "possibly canon".

Paradox Space was also released in book form, in two volumes, each containing a few physical-only stories, available here because both books are permanently out of stock.

Read Paradox Space

Paid Works

Homestuck Book Releases

Released by VIZ Media, these books were a reboot of the Topatoco book releases, this time reaching the Act 5 Act 2 Scratch intermission.

The books adapted Homestuck to a static format, contained commentary written by Andrew Hussie on every single page, and were the ideal way of directly supporting Homestuck.

As of 2024, VIZ has lost the Homestuck license, and there is no alternative book release yet. The Homestuck Collection with the commentary mod is the recommended way to get the same experience. Archive links to the volume releases are included below.

Sweet Bro and Hella Jeff Book Releases

The first of these two Topatoco books is a compilation of all the SBAHJ comics, with some commentary and bonuses written in-character by Dave Strider. The second is an original story written by @dril and drawn by KC Green, widely accepted to be disappointing.

  • Sweet Bro and Hella Jeff Limited Hardcover (Discontinued)
  • Sweet Bro and Hella Jeff and the Quest for the Missing Spoon

HIVESWAP

A series of possibly-canonical point-and-click games set in the Homestuck universe. Play as Joey Claire as she accidentally gets hive-swapped with a Troll from another universe.

After the 2012 Homestuck Kickstarter, HIVESWAP ACT 1, the first installment, was released 2017. HIVESWAP ACT 2 was released three years later.
